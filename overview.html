About This Course

Issues such as the return of items of cultural patrimony, the looting and annihilation of irreplaceable cultural heritage monuments, traditional cultural properties and the desecration of national heritage sites worldwide plague our world daily. It is my hope that an online, digital class featuring both United States and UNESCO policies will enhance cultural heritage management practices as well as deliver needed policy training for people in institutions such as public or tribal museums, and government offices. This need has been evident at every consultation I have attended. I chose an online format for this course because the laws dealt with are tedious in a standard lecture format. This module format allows for the topics to be broken down into a series of informational online sessions that appeal to a wide range of disciplines and audiences.

This online course specializes in cultural heritage management policy and law both nationally and internationally. The design of this course is to assist local communities with the preservation policy protocol to disseminate their own cultural heritage agendas to a wider range of recipients. As digital outlets become the most common way to reach the widest audience, it is crucial that cultural managers take advantage of this trend. In an era where funding quickly disappears without an apparent real-world application and sites are destroyed before mitigation plans can be implemented, it is crucial this knowledge reaches a wide audience and a broad market.

This course will highlight some of the more notorious cases, how they were dealt with and the applicable laws used in their mitigation. This will be a mixed methods course, involving short lectures on each policy, when the policy applies and the steps to work through it. After each lecture, hands on activities involving actual cases from around the world will be used to allow you to work through the mitigation of each law. Short lectures, discussing how each case was mitigated, the strengths and weaknesses of these policies, and the results of each mitigation will also be included. Our Share Your Stories section will be used to stimulate interactive discussions about what things went both right and wrong in these mitigations as well as allowing a space for participants to share their own stories and unique mitigation strategies.
